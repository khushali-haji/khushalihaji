Hello. I'm Khushali, a researcher, multimedia journalist and filmmaker, interested in how people experience systems, cities, and power, and how storytelling can bring data and policy to life.

I trained as an urban designer at CEPT University in India, learning how cities are planned on paper through land-use, zoning, and infrastructure. But what stayed with me were the things that didn't quite fit on those pages: the messiness of everyday life, the politics operating at different scales, and the emotions, negotiations, and aspirations of the people who actually live within those lines. I kept asking: Who is the city really for? Who gets left out? And how do people navigate systems that weren't built with them in mind?

Looking for ways to explore those questions, I turned to local theater, research essays, and character-driven fiction. Storytelling became a way to research – to listen closely, sit with contradiction, and bring lived experience into abstract or technical conversations. I later studied International Reporting and Documentary Filmmaking at the Newmark Graduate School of Journalism (CUNY), where I learned to follow stories in the space between policy, data, and lived reality, and use different media to tell them.

Since then, my work has moved across formats and geographies, from investigative reporting on carbon credit markets in Liberia, to coverage of asylum policy and public health in the U.S., to community engagement and research for a crowd-management project in Varanasi. I've reported on issues ranging from domestic violence to interfaith community-building, and made short films about connection, ambition, and belonging. My reporting has been recognized by the Silurians Press Club, and my short documentary received a Special Jury Mention at the 2025 Woodstock Film Festival.

Now based in New York, I work across journalism, video, and research-driven storytelling, always drawn to projects where systems meet lived experience. I'm especially interested in roles that sit between urban planning, public policy, and content or narrative strategy, work that involves research, creative engagement, and communicating complex ideas clearly and responsibly.
