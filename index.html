Hello. I'm Khushali, a researcher, multimedia journalist and filmmaker, interested in how people experience systems, cities, and power, and how storytelling can bring data and policy to life.
I trained as an urban designer in Ahmedabad, India, learning to draw cities through land use, zoning, infrastructure. But what stayed with me was everything that didn't fit: the informal, the political, the socio-economic, the human. I turned to theater, research essays, and eventually journalism to get closer to that.
Since my MA in International Reporting and Documentary Filmmaking at CUNY Newmark, I've reported on carbon credit fraud in Liberia, asylum policy in the US, and studied community politics in India. I've edited indie documentaries, set up DCPs at a post house, led content strategy and community engagement, and am now researching international conflict stories for a mapping startup, plotting geopolitical narrative onto a live map API.
Based in New York, I'm seeking roles at the intersection of spatial data, narrative strategy, and public-interest storytelling, where research meets design, and complexity is the point.
